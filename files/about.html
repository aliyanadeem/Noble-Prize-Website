What is the Nobel Prize?

The Nobel Prize is one of the most prestigious awards in the world, established by Alfred Nobel in 1895. It is awarded annually in six categories: Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences. The prize honors individuals and organizations that have made outstanding contributions to humanity.

The first Nobel Prizes were awarded in 1901, and since then, the ceremony has become a symbol of excellence and global recognition.
